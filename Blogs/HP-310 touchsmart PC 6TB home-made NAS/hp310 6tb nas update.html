What is a NAS?

A NAS stands for: Network Attached Storage, and it's basically an external hard drive but smarter.
As I stated in my video, a NAS is essentially a computer that has a CPU, RAM, a motherboard, a host drive, and external hard drives that you attach to it. Many consumer NAS units come with 2, 4, or 6 hard drive bays, allowing you to add multiple hard drives for more storage or redundancy. You can connect multiple devices like: your phone, tablet, and computer to it from your local network or anywhere in the world if you set up port forwarding or a VPN, but be safe and do it securely.


What did I change this time?

To be honest, not much really. I just changed the computer from my Toshiba Satellite L455-S5980 to my HP-310 Touchsmart AIO PC and moved the hard drives around for a backup. The NAS is now 6TB instead of 12TB. I use the 2.5 external 2TB and a 4TB drives for the NAS and I use the 6TB Expansion drive as the backup.
