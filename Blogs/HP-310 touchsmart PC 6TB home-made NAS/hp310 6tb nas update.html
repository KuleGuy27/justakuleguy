What is a NAS?

A NAS stands for: Network Attached Storage, and it's basically an external hard drive but smarter.
As I stated in my video, a NAS is essentially a computer that has a CPU, RAM, a motherboard, a host drive, and external hard drives that you attach to it. Many consumer NAS units come with 2, 4, or 6 hard drive bays, allowing you to add multiple hard drives for more storage or redundancy. You can connect multiple devices like: your phone, tablet, and computer to it from your local network or anywhere in the world if you set up port forwarding or a VPN, but be safe and do it securely.

What OS do I use for the NAS?

Same as the one in the video, I will be using Ubuntu Server and CasaOS.

Ubuntu Server - https://ubuntu.com/download/server
CasaOS - https://casaos.zimaspace.com/

There are tons of NAS OSes out there, like: TrueNAS Core/Scale, OpenMediaVault, Proxmox, Unraid, and etc... Those are really good operating systems and I have dabbled with them in the past, but I will using Ubuntu Server and CasaOS because of familiarity and ease of use. I have been on these 2 OSes for more than 1 year and I'm pretty familiar with it. CasaOS makes it easy to use by having a GUI interface and so far, I love it!
